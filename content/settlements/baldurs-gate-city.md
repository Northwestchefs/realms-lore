# Baldur's Gate (City)

## Overview
A major port city marked by intense class contrast, competitive commerce, and persistent faction pressure.

## Role in the Realm
Primary settlement of [[nations-realms/baldurs-gate|Baldur's Gate]], where governance, trade, and criminal economies overlap.

## Governance and Power
- Civic authority is shared across elite offices, legal councils, and district-level enforcement.
- Wealthy commercial blocs can shape policy through contracts, loans, and shipping control.
- Public order is often negotiated in practice rather than uniformly imposed.

## District and Landmark Use
- Harbor wards for cargo theft, customs disputes, and smuggling investigations.
- Merchant quarters for contract intrigue, debt leverage, and guild competition.
- Outer neighborhoods for refugee tensions, recruitment drives, and street-level faction deals.

## Economy and Daily Life
- Port handling, warehousing, finance, and caravan staging drive most civic priorities.
- Sharp wealth gaps create different legal realities between neighborhoods.
- Taverns, shrines, and market courts are reliable rumor and patron hubs.

## Immediate Adventure Value
- Excellent for street-level intrigue, corruption investigations, and faction-brokered negotiations.
- Works well for legal drama where evidence and witnesses matter as much as combat.
- Supports downtime-focused arcs tied to property, trade, and social reputation.

## Hooks
- A magistrate hires discreet outsiders to prove a forged shipping license ring before riots begin.
- A dockworkers' dispute masks a larger struggle between lenders and caravan syndicates.
- A neighborhood protection pact collapses, and three factions compete to "restore order" first.
